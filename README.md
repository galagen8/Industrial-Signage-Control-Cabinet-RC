# Industrial-Signage-Control-Cabinet-RC
Electrical control cabinet and power distribution system for a high-power outdoor LED signage system, featuring IEC-compliant thermal and voltage drop calculations.

## 1. Project Overview
* **Application:** High-power LED Signage System.
* **Compliance & Standards:** IEC Regulations for voltage drop, heat dissipation, and cable sizing.
* **Key Challenges:** Managing high inrush currents from multiple parallel power supplies, ensuring active thermal cooling within a sealed industrial enclosure, and calculating precise linear power dissipation over long cable runs.

---

## 2. Technical Specifications & Features

### Power & Automation Control
* **Grid Input:** 220VAC Single-Phase with comprehensive voltage monitoring (via `RS PRO 122-215` relay) to protect downstream electronics.
* **Automated Scheduling:** Integrated digital timer clock (`THC15A`) coupled with a heavy-duty 22A contactor (`LG GMC-22`) for automated day/night cycling.

### Inrush Current & Safety Management
* **Inrush Protection:** Equipped with a MeanWell `ICL-16R` Inrush Current Limiter to suppress the massive simultaneous startup current ($120A$ peak) generated by the PSU array, preventing nuisance tripping of the 16A Type-C MCB.
* **Circuit Protection:** Discretized protection layers using ABB Type-C miniature circuit breakers (16A for main power, 6A for auxiliary control circuits).

### Thermal & Cabinet Engineering
* **Active Cooling:** Forced-air ventilation using a 220VAC enclosure fan.
* **Thermal Dissipation Design:** Adherence to strict placement guidelines (5mm lateral clearance for components) and direct chassis-to-grounded-backplane mounting PSUs for optimal thermal sinking and EMC mitigation.

---

## 3. Engineering Calculations & Simulations

### Cable Sizing & Linear Power Dissipation (IEC Compliance)
To guarantee system reliability and avoid critical voltage drops across extensive cable runs (up to 50 meters), rigorous analytical math was applied based on wire surface area ($S = \pi \cdot d \cdot L$) and heat transfer coefficients:

* **Voltage Drop Formula:**
  $$V_{drop} = I \times \left(0.0175 \times \frac{2L}{A}\right)$$
* **Temperature Rise Calculation:**
  $$\Delta T = \frac{P}{\alpha \times S}$$

#### Calculation Results Analysis:
The project evaluates three different cable cross-sections (`10mm²`, `16mm²`, and `25mm²`) to optimize cost vs. performance. 
* *Decision:* **LAPP H07V-K 1x16** was selected for long trunk lines as it limits temperature rise to a safe $\Delta T \approx 1.52^\circ\text{C}$ and cuts power loss (voltage drop) significantly compared to 10mm² alternatives.
